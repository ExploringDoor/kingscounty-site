1
Eligibility to Play
Important

To be eligible to play on any team in the KCSL, a player must:

  • Be Jewish or a member of a Jewish household
  • Be 21 years of age or older, OR be an "Underage Player" (16+) with a parent on the same roster and in the lineup for any game in which they play
  • Comply with at least ONE of the following:
    • Be a full-time dues-paying member of a synagogue
    • Have a child in a Jewish Day School program
    • Be a full-time employee of Goldstein's
    • Make a verifiable charitable donation of $100+ to a Jewish charity or organization
Underage Players (16–21): Eligibility requirements under section (c) may be satisfied by the player's parent(s). All players aged 16–21 must register via imleagues.com. Team Captains must notify Commissioners of any such registrations.

1b. Eligibility Enforcement

If a team believes an opposing team has used an ineligible player, the Captain must submit a confidential email to the Commissioners including the alleged player's name, games played, and supporting information. The Commissioners will:

  • Notify the Offending Team's Captain, who has 48 hours to provide evidence of eligibility
  • Convene a Peer Review Group of 3 neutral Captains (anonymous) to review the evidence
  • The Peer Review Group renders a decision within 24 hours of receiving evidence
  • The player may continue to play while the review is pending, but if found ineligible, the Offending Team forfeits all games played after notification

1c. Penalties

  • First Offense: Forfeit all games in which the ineligible player participated
  • Second Offense: Team is ineligible for playoffs that season. Team Captain is banned from serving as Captain in the KCSL
2
Modified Fast Pitch League

All KCSL games are classified as "Modified Fast Pitch" as defined by current ASA Rules. Slingshot, windmill, and high arc pitching are all considered illegal.

3
Roster
Deadline: May 31

Each team must submit a roster to the League Office by May 31 each year. Maximum roster size is 25 players. Any roster changes after May 31 require Commissioner approval.

Penalty: Failure to submit a roster by May 31 results in forfeiture of every game played after that date until a roster is received.
4
Official Game

All KCSL games are 7 innings. A game is declared official if at least 5 innings have been played and the home team has batted if they were behind in the bottom of the 5th.

5–6
Game Equipment

Home Team must supply:

  • 3 bases, home plate, catcher's mask
  • 2 new softballs, 3 orange cones, pitcher's rubber
  • Orange/white extension for the back of home plate
  • A score book

Visiting Team must supply:

  • A score book and 2 new softballs (backup balls)

All teams must also have the KCSL Rulebook, ASA Rulebook, a rake, and a shovel.

7–9
Game Play & Special Rules

Field Players: Maximum 10 players in the field, minimum 8 at any time. Fewer than 8 is a forfeit.

Batting Order: All rostered players in attendance must be placed in the batting order. Any player arriving after the first pitch goes at the end of the lineup (waivable by mutual consent). If a batting spot becomes vacant due to injury or early departure, that spot is simply skipped — no out is charged.

Ejected Player: If a player is ejected, their spot is deemed vacant and the team is charged with an automatic out each time that spot comes up.

All players must play the field for at least one full inning. Penalty for failure to bat or field (unless injured): Forfeit of the game.

Special Field Rules

  • Third Base Cone: A cone is placed halfway between 3rd and home. All plays at home plate are force outs. Once a runner passes the cone, they are committed to run home. Turning back = automatic out.
  • Home Plate Extension: Fielder must tag the white portion; runner must touch the orange/white extension. The runner must avoid contact. Catcher may also tag the runner.
  • 1st Base Double Bag: Fielder must tag the white portion; runner may use the orange portion to avoid contact. Where a play at first is likely, runner must use the orange side only.
  • No Metal Cleats — ever. Penalty: immediate ejection.
  • No team may force another team to play an 8:00 PM game at Southampton.
  • Forfeit fee: If your team forfeits, you owe the $50 umpire's fee to the KCSL.

Pitching Rules

Pitching distance: 46 feet. If the field has a built-in rubber shorter than 46', both teams may agree to pitch from that distance; if one team wants 46', both must comply.

Allowable pitching modifications (as long as no slingshot or windmill):

  • Pitcher may start with one foot on the rubber
  • Pitcher may rock back and come forward on the release
  • No restriction on backswing — arm can go as high as possible
  • The pitcher does not have to pitch on an intentional walk

Base Running Rules

  • No leads or stealing. All runners may leave bases on the release of the pitch.
  • All runners remain in jeopardy to be picked off until they return to their base.
  • Runners may advance on errant pick-off throws (overthrown or not cleanly fielded on first stab). All runners must tag up before advancing.
  • In case of collision due to an errant pick-off throw: dead ball, no runners advance.
8
Umpiring

Umpiring is performed by the designated professional umpiring crew. All umpire decisions are final.

If no professional umpire is present at game time, each team designates a rostered player to officiate while their team is batting. By mutual captain consent, both teams may designate one player to officiate for both sides.

Non-members of the KCSL are not permitted to officiate or to be on the field during any KCSL games.
10
Darkness & Weather

Games may be called for darkness or weather only at the umpire's sole discretion. If stopped mid-inning after 5 innings, the score reverts to the last fully completed inning.

Example: Home team leads 6–5 going into the top of the 6th. Visitors take the lead in the 6th, then lightning stops play. Score reverts to end of 5th — home team wins 6–5.

All make-up games start from the top of the 1st inning — they do not resume from where they left off.

11–12
Postponements & Rescheduling

A game may be postponed if: the field is deemed unfit by Commissioners or Upper Dublin Township; the umpire rules the field unplayable; or both captains mutually agree at least 48 hours in advance.

Reschedule deadline: Any postponed game must be rescheduled within 48 hours of postponement and played within 21 days of the original date. If captains fail to do so, Commissioners will assign the date and location.

If a team cannot field a team, the captain must notify the opponent at least 48 hours in advance to reschedule. Failure to do so results in a forfeit.

Non-rostered player: Playing a non-rostered player results in an immediate forfeit of that game.
13
Starting Times
  • April: 6:00 PM with 15-minute grace period
  • May onward: 6:15 PM with 15-minute grace period
  • All playoff games: 6:00 PM with 15-minute grace period

Games begin on time if both teams have at least 8 players.

14
Reporting Scores

Captains of both teams must report scores within 24 hours of each game:

  • Via imleagues.com
  • Via email to [email protected] — put "score" in the subject line and the actual score in the body
16
Courtesy Base Runners

Each team is permitted a maximum of 3 courtesy runners per game. Additional courtesy runners are only allowed if a player is clearly and evidently injured during the game.

  • The courtesy runner must be the player who made the last batted out in that inning or the preceding inning
  • Players designated for courtesy runners must be identified to the opposing captain before the first pitch
  • Once a player uses a courtesy runner, they must continue using one for the rest of the game
The intent of courtesy runners is to assist players who are legitimately physically injured or challenged — not to gain a speed advantage.
17–18
Conduct & Fighting
Zero Tolerance

Slide or Surrender: Runners must slide or surrender at any base to avoid a collision. Intentional interference = automatic out + ejection + 2-game suspension (must be reported within 24 hours).

Fighting: Any fighting during, immediately before, or after a game results in automatic expulsion from the League for the season. A player may apply for reinstatement the following year. The KCSL reserves the right to suspend or terminate any player or team.

The bottom line... cooler heads must prevail!

20
Post-Season Playoffs
Updated
  • Teams 1–6: Automatically qualify for the Gold Championship
  • Teams 7–10: Play a one-game elimination to determine the last 2 Gold seeds and first 2 Silver seeds
  • Teams 15–18: Play to determine the last 2 Silver Championship seeds
  • Team 19: Eliminated

Semi-finals and the League Championship game will have 2 umpires. Commissioners and umpire-in-chief select all playoff umpires.

Playoff weather: Unlike regular season, playoff games are fully played through at least 7 innings. If called for weather, the game resumes exactly where it left off at the next scheduled playoff date.
22
Permitted Bats
No Metal
No metal, composite, aluminum, or non-wooden bats of any kind. ALL wooden softball bats are permitted regardless of ASA labeling, provided they meet ASA dimensional standards.

Exception: Female players may use metal, aluminum, or composite bats, provided the bat is not otherwise banned under ASA or KCSL rules, and is not a "triple wall" bat.

Only captains may question or challenge a suspected illegal bat. No player has the right to inspect the opposing team's bat bag. The umpire makes the final determination.

23
KCSL Commissioners

The KCSL is governed by 3 Commissioners plus a Treasurer. By majority vote, Commissioners resolve all disputes, interpret and enforce KCSL rules, and impose all penalties. Their collective decision is final and binding. Vacancies are filled by appointment of the remaining Commissioners.

24
Two Forfeits Rule
Playoff Disqualification
Two or more forfeits during a season results in forfeiture of the team's playoff spot and possible additional disciplinary action the following season.
26
Jewish Holidays

No team shall be required to play — regular season or playoff — on a Jewish holiday, regardless of how minor. Captains must exercise good faith to reschedule any such conflict at the next available date.

27
Field / Park Rules
Zero Tolerance
  • No alcohol or tobacco of any kind at any KCSL game — applies to all players, family members, and spectators. Captains are responsible for enforcement.
  • Players may not change in their cars at the fields. Use appropriate facilities.
  • Absolutely NO DOGS permitted on or at any field under any circumstances — applies to family and friends as well.
  • Any fine from a field, park, or township rule violation is passed directly to the offending team's captain, plus an additional $25 KCSL fine. All fines must be paid within 5 days or the team forfeits all subsequent games until paid.
28
Mercy Rule

If, after completing at least 5 full innings, a team leads by 15 or more runs, the game is called — provided the trailing team has had its at-bats. If the deficit falls below 15 as a result of that at-bat, the game continues normally.

30
Player Ejection
Mandatory Suspension
  • An ejected player must physically leave not only the playing field but the entire grounds including the parking lot. Failure to leave results in game forfeit for that player's team.
  • Any ejected player is automatically suspended from the next game. They may not attend or appear near that game's field.
  • A second ejection in a season results in indefinite suspension pending official reinstatement by the Commissioners via formal written application.
33
Uniforms

The KCSL Logo must be printed on all team jerseys, prominently and tastefully displayed, conforming to current Commissioner-approved design. All players must wear a team jersey with a unique uniform number when playing.

34
Changing Teams

Any player wishing to change teams must notify the Commissioners, who will make that player eligible via the KCSL draft process, starting with the last-place team from the prior season. This rule is designed to prevent "team hopping."

Intra-Synagogue Transfers: Where a synagogue has multiple teams, a player may switch only with the full mutual consent of all captains involved. Disputes are resolved by assigning the player to the team with fewer wins from the prior season.

35
Designated Pitcher Rule
2025

In effect for all KCSL games — regular season, play-in, and playoffs. Requires 12+ players in the batting lineup to invoke. Teams with 11 or fewer players cannot use this rule.

  • A team with 12+ players may bat 12+ and use an additional 13th player as a Designated Pitcher
  • The Designated Pitcher has no spot in the batting order — no out is charged for not batting
  • The DP cannot enter the batting order at any point during the game
  • The DP may only appear as a pitcher and may not play any other defensive position
  • The DP need not pitch every inning and may be substituted in and out, but substitutions must come from the 12-player starting lineup
  • Use of the Designated Pitcher Rule is determined on a game-by-game basis
Teams must inform their opponent's Captain during the pre-game Ground Rules conference if they intend to use a Designated Pitcher, and must designate the DP on their Lineup card.

Ground rules do not supersede the official rulebook. Umpires hold final authority.
The following ground rules govern play at all KCSL venues for the 2025 season. Each venue presents unique physical characteristics — walkways, berms, trees, and adjacent property — requiring specific rulings beyond the standard rulebook. Umpires hold final authority on all ground rule disputes during play.

Mondauk Common
1st BaseRight Field Line
90° perpendicular line from the end of the dugout fence to the base of the berm/hill, continuing straight in line with the base of the berm.
3rd BaseLeft Field Line
Line from end of dugout fence to the point on the walk path where the path curves to the right.
Special Rules
Home RunA batted ball that rolls onto the walk path in Left Field is ruled a home run.
Fly BallIf a fly ball strikes the overhanging tree in Left Field, ball is immediately dead. If it would have been foul — charged a foul ball. If fair — no penalty; ball is replayed.
Thrown BallIf a thrown ball strikes the overhanging tree in Left Field, ball remains live. All runners may advance at will.

GR DoubleA fair batted ball entering the swale or crossing the walk path down the RF line is dead. Each runner awarded 2 bases from position when hit.
